The Mediterranean diet

The Mediterranean diet is based on foods that have historically been consumed in places like Italy and Greece. It is abundant in fruits and veggies, whole grains lentils, salmon, almonds, and olive oil Red meat consumption should be restricted, while foods like chicken, eggs, and dairy products should be consumed in moderation. The Mediterranean diet also restricts: refined foods trans fat added sugar, processed meats, and other highly processed foods This diet's focus on minimally processed foods and plants has been linked to a lower risk of developing a number of chronic diseases as well as a longer life span. Additionally, studies demonstrate that the Mediterranean diet prevents several malignancies (1Trusted Source). Numerous studies show that the diet's plant-based, high unsaturated fat nutritional pattern can help with weight loss, despite the fact that it was created to reduce the risk of heart disease (2).
